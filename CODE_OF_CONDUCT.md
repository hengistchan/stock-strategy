# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to making participation in Strategy Lab a respectful, harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of background, identity, experience level, or perspective.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, constructive, and specific.
- Assume good intent while discussing evidence and technical trade-offs.
- Welcome newcomers and help them reproduce results.
- Keep discussions focused on software, data quality, and documented backtest behavior.
- Respect privacy and never request account credentials, OpenD passwords, or private trading data.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, or personal attacks.
- Publishing another person's private information without permission.
- Misrepresenting backtest results as guaranteed returns or personalized investment advice.
- Deliberately introducing hidden network access, credential collection, or live-order behavior.
- Repeated disruption of project discussions or review processes.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may edit or remove contributions that violate this policy and may temporarily or permanently restrict participation. Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, documented privately when appropriate, and focused on protecting the community.

Report conduct concerns privately to the project owner through the contact options on the maintainer's GitHub profile. Security vulnerabilities must use GitHub's private vulnerability reporting flow described in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions, code review, and other spaces where someone is representing the project.
